CHEESE!

So this page might seem fairly random just stumbling across it on my page. So let me explain. While I was at work, my coworkers and I starting talking about cheese. Next, we started telling each other our favorite types of cheese. Then we moved to looking up random cheese facts to tell each other. Weird, I know. But we're crazy college students. What do you expect? So here are some cheese facts to blow your mind!

Facts

  1. We'll start off with a basic one. The word "cheese" comes from the Latin word "caseus" which means "to ferment" or "to sour".
  2. A person who sells cheese is known as a "cheesemonger".
  3. The ancient Greek hero Aristaeus is credited for the discovery of feta cheese.
  4. Collecting cheese labels is called "tyrosemiophilia".
  5. A giant wheel of Cheddar cheese was given to Queen Victoria (1837-1901) for a wedding gift. It weighed over 1,000 pounds. A normal Cheddar wheel weighs 60-75 pounds.
  6. In the United States the month of June is National Dairy Month and the last week of June is National Cheese Week.
  7. A benefit associated with cheese is that it helps protect tooth enamel and has an antibacterial effect. If consumed in moderate quantities it has various health benefits.
  8. Pizza Hut is the largest cheese user. They use 30 million pounds of cheese annually--mostly on pizza.
  9. In the Roman empire, houses had separate kitchens for producing cheese. These were called "careale".
  10. The most stolen food item in the world is cheese. Four percent of all cheese sold ends up being stolen.
  11. When cheese is digested, it breaks down into an opiod. Common opiods are herion and morphine.
  12. Stilton blue cheese is known to frequently cause odd, vivid dreams.
  13. Scientists have successfully created cheese using human bacteria collected from toes and belly buttons.
  14. The French have a cheese for every day of the year.
  15. Cheese existed before written language did.
  16. Every pound of cheese requires ten pounds of milk.
